  • 10/28/2020

    Initial report

    The Allegheny County Health Department received a report of "bathroom ceiling collapsed from upstairs leak," plus leaks in the kitchen and around the base of the toilet and a "mice infestation." The department noted elsewhere that the household included two children under the age of six.

  • 1/13/2021

    First inspection

    An inspector reported that the heat was inoperable, and the tenant was using a gas stove to provide heat. Ceiling panels were missing, and there were holes in the wall. The kitchen faucet ran continually, and the cabinet was rotting. The shared laundry room and shared stairways were littered with trash, and an area of shared hallway that had been the scene of a sewer backup had not been disinfected.

  • 1/15/2021

    First violations

    The department issued a Notice of Violation based on the inspection two days earlier, demanding that the lack of heat be addressed within six days and the other violations within five weeks.

  • 1/15/2021

    Escrow option

    The department also authorized the tenant to pay rent into an escrow account rather than paying the landlord.

  • 2/11/2021

    Tenant unavailable

    After the tenant was not home at the time of a scheduled inspection, the inspector tried, but failed, to reach the tenant to schedule another inspection.

  • 2/12/2021

    Second inspection

    An inspector found that the heating system was still inoperable, and the tenant was continuing to heat the apartment using the stove. Also unaddressed: the rotting cabinet, holes in the wall and ceiling, and the sewage backup contamination that had not been disinfected. The inspector left a voice mail for property manager Preservation Management Inc. [PMI] but was unable to issue a penalty warning letter because of an information technology problem.

  • 2/16/2021

    Penalty threat

    The department issued a letter warning that, if the violations were not abated within six days, the landlord would face a civil penalty of $2,500, plus $250 per day that the violations continued. (There is no record that any penalty was actually assessed.)

  • 3/5/2021

    Tenant no-show

    An inspector reported that the tenant wasn't on hand, preventing a scheduled reinspection.

  • 3/19/2021

    Third inspection

    An inspector found that several violations — inoperable heat (except for a space heater), rot below the kitchen sink, failure to disinfect — still had not been addressed.

  • 3/23/2021

    Landlord call

    A department official called a PMI senior regional manager, who said she thought the heat was working and blamed the tenant for "turning a valve off by mistake."

  • 3/26/2021

    Fourth inspection

    An inspector found that the cabinet below the kitchen sink continued to rot, and the sewage backup still had not been disinfected, but did not note any further problem with the heat.

  • 3/29/2021

    Minor violations

    The department issued a letter asking PMI to give "immediate attention" to remaining violations but noted that they "do not represent a serious health hazard" and indicated that it "plans no further action at this time."

  • 4/23/2021

    Case closed

    The department closed the case.
